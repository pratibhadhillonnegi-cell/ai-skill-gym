Why Most Prompts Fail (And How to Fix Them)

Published: May 10, 2026

Most beginners write prompts like they are searching Google. They use vague keywords and expect the AI to read their mind.

The Problem: Lack of Constraints

When you ask "Write a marketing email for my product", you give the AI zero boundaries. It will guess the tone, the length, the audience, and the goal.

The Fix: The 4-Part Structure

  1. Role: "Act as a senior copywriter."
  2. Task: "Write a 3-paragraph email promoting our new AI tool."
  3. Context/Audience: "The audience is busy founders who want to save time."
  4. Format/Constraints: "Keep it punchy, use short sentences, no emojis."
